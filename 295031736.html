New IT Delivery Patterns : AWS Analytics 2: Cleanup

Created by Karina Jonane, last modified by Thomas R. Evans on May 12, 2021

This is a required step upon completion of the AWS Analytics 2 Ah Hoc exercise and an important point of operational awareness.   Many of the artifacts and tools you create on AWS do not clean themselves up.  Left on their own, they will continue to accrue charges.  Currently, Accenture is helping large clients troubleshoot their spend on AWS as many organizations find out that their developers are leaving remnants out on AWS.  For some clients, this can add up into millions of dollars of wasted cost in unused resources.

QuickSight Cleanup

  • Toggle back to QuickSight.

  • Click the QuickSight icon in the upper-left of the screen.


Delete your Analysis

  1. Choose Analyses.
  2. Click the vertical elipsis then choose Delete and confirm deletion when prompted.




Delete your Dataset

  1. Choose Datasets.
  2. Choose product_marketing_data, then choose Delete dataset at the resulting popup dialog.



    (The remaining datasets were created as samples upon your Quicksight account creation. There is no need to delete those as part of this cleanup.

Athena Cleanup

There is no Athena clean-up to do in the scope of the exercise as documented, because the only Athena activity related to the Glue database and crawler.

Glue Cleanup

Delete Glue database(s)

  • Access the AWS Glue service.
  1. Select Databases in the left-hand navigation area, tick the check box next to the database(s) you created as part of this exercise and click Delete database.
  2. Click Delete once more at the confirmation prompt.

Delete Glue Crawler(s)

  1. Select Crawlers in the left-hand navigation area, tick the check box next to the crawler(s) you created as part of this exercise and click Delete crawler.
  2. Click Delete once more at the confirmation prompt.

S3 Cleanup

There is no S3 clean-up to do in the scope of the exercise as documented, because the bucket and file you used is shared by all account users.
